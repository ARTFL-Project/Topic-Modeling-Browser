Palais-Bourbon , 2 août 1775 Begs leave to defend Voltaire, who has been his friend for forty years. He is being persecuted because of a work against religion attributed to him. Enraged priests clamour for the ashes of this . . . vieux destructeur de l'autel et du trône . . . — and the little grenadiers of the hierarchy enflame the anger of the most esteemed prelates. Without the protection of Malesherbes, for which he appeals, the Nestor of sages would be obliged to drag out his old age on foreign soil. It is permissible to criticize Voltaire's works, but the riff-raff of literature should not outrage his person nor band together to lead him to the place de Grève.
