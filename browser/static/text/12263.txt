Paris , 13 février 1749 . . . J'ai perdu un an en Lorraine où il est impossible de travailler au milleu de la dissipation et de la vie coupée qu'on y mène. She is writing the preface to her translation of Newton, and is adding a discussion of the earth, the moon and the new discoveries of Clairault. The astronomer Pierre Lemonnier appears to her to be better as an observer than as a geometrician. Refers to Algarotti, to Voltaire, and to her son's stay in Rome.
